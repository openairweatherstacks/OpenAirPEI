# Town partnership outreach — sequencing

This folder contains pitch artifacts for OpenAir Atlantic town partnerships. Each artifact has a specific use and a specific *time window* in which it should be sent. **Sending the wrong artifact at the wrong moment is worse than sending nothing.**

### Why the hold on the CAO pitch

The previous Stratford CAO Jeremy Crosby passed away suddenly on March 9, 2026. The position has been vacant since. Sending a cold partnership pitch into a vacant CAO inbox during a transition period:

1. Sits unread for weeks
2. Reads as opportunistic if it lands during the grief/transition window
3. Gets routed to the wrong person and burns the future relationship

Wait for the new CAO appointment. Track via [Town of Stratford CAO Department](https://www.townofstratford.ca/government/about_our_government/departments/cao_department) and PEI municipal news sources.

### Why the comms email can go now

Communications/Marketing/RCE staff are still in their roles. They own the website day-to-day. Embedding a free widget or adding a link does not require CAO sign-off in most PEI municipalities — a comms officer can ship it. Engaging now also pre-seeds the eventual CAO conversation: the new CAO arrives and finds *"we're already partnered with this OpenAir thing"* in their handover notes.

## Recurring rules across all town pitches

These apply to Stratford and to every future town outreach (Charlottetown, Summerside, Cornwall, etc.):

1. **Verify the recipient is alive and in their role before sending.** Use the town's staff directory + a recent news search. PEI municipalities have small staffs and high turnover; assumptions go stale fast.
2. **Lead with what's free, what's already built, and what asks nothing of them.** Save formal asks (partnership, sponsorship, co-branding) for after a relationship exists.
3. **Don't quote their Strategic Plan KPIs in the first email.** Looks like research-stalking. Save it for the formal CAO/Council pitch where it strengthens the ask.
4. **Don't ask for a meeting in the first email.** Meetings are a cost; embedding a widget is free.
5. **Single follow-up only.** One nudge after 10 business days, then drop it. The page keeps serving residents whether the town engages or not.
6. **No attachments in the cold email.** The email IS the pitch. Attachments get filtered, ignored, or look spammy.
7. **Re-verify everything after 60 days.** Staff change, contact details rot, organizations restructure. If a pitch artifact is more than two months old, re-check the staff directory and recent news before sending.

## When OpenAir Atlantic adds a new town to /town/[slug]

Before writing any pitch, fill out:

1. **Comms staff target** — name, role, email (verified in the last 30 days)
2. **CAO name + appointment date** (verified)
3. **The town's strategic plan or equivalent council priorities document** — for KPI mapping in the formal pitch
4. **One concrete service-first hook unique to that town** — for Stratford it's "we send 40+ links to townofstratford.ca"; for Cavendish it would be "we send tourism traffic to the Parks Canada Cavendish page"; for Summerside it would be different again

Then duplicate the Stratford pair (`-comms-email.md` + `-cao-pitch.md`) and customize. The structure is intentionally generic; the hooks must be specific.

## Tracking sent outreach

When you send a pitch, log it inline at the top of the artifact file with: date sent, recipient name + email, response received (with date), outcome. Keeps the history with the artifact instead of in a separate spreadsheet that goes stale.

Example:
```
**Sent log:**
- 2026-05-20 → emailed [Name] at [email] → no response after 10d
- 2026-06-03 → followed up once → polite decline, files in their backlog for fall review
```
